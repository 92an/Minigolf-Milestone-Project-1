How to play!

  1. The game is played across 18 tracks.
  2. It can be played individually or in a group.
  3. The total limit of swings per track i 7, the one with the least swings wins.
  4. The maximum number of swings is 126 and the least is 18.
  5. The ball has to be placed in the designated possition at the start.
  6. Some tracks have checkpoints you have to clear before you can proceed midway.
  7. If such a checkpoints has passed you may place the ball the size of the golfclub putter away from the rim of the track.
  8. if the ball goes out of the track it starts at the beginning
  9. Certain tracks are made of concrete and you MAY NOT stand on them

About the club


Miniature golf was first introduced in the US during the 1910. It was from there that Edwin Norrman and Eskil Norman brought the idea to Sweden after their travels there. They started Norrman and Norman Minatyrgolf in 1931. In the early days of miniature golf, the tracks were constructed using wood and tennis sand, unlike the American tracks that used felt. In the 1960s the felt material became immensely popular in Sweden and is today the most common type of material that is used. An interesting fact is that the Swedish minigolf federation was founded in 1939 making it one of the oldest, if not the oldest, minigolf sports organization in the world.

There are today 4 different type of tracks currently standardized courses supported by the World Minigolfsport Federation (WMF)


  • Beton
  • Eternite
  • Felt
  • Minigolf Open Standard

Our Values


We at Miniature Golf Adventure embrace the value and ethical standards of the World Minigolf Sports Federation. Integrity as sportsmen to ensure that we play fair and as such the game is fun for all. Respect of our opponents and fellow golfers to ensure the safety and wellbeing of all golfers. Passion for the sport and enthusiasm for competition and play. Connecting generations as this is a sport for all ages, religions, and backgrounds.
